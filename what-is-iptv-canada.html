IPTV (Internet Protocol Television) is a method of delivering television channels over the internet instead of through a cable or satellite connection. In Canada, IPTV allows you to watch CBC, CTV, TSN, Sportsnet, RDS, and 25,000+ channels on any device — TV, phone, or tablet — starting from $12.99 CAD/month. Bell, Rogers, and Telus all use IPTV technology to deliver their own cable TV services.

How IPTV Works

Traditional cable TV sends signals through coaxial cables buried in the ground. IPTV sends those same TV channels as data packets over your home internet connection — the same way Netflix or YouTube delivers video.

The result: you get the same live channels (CBC, CTV, TSN, NHL games, news) but without a cable box, without contracts, and at a fraction of the price. You watch on a Firestick, Smart TV, phone, or laptop.

Is IPTV Legal in Canada?

Yes — IPTV technology is completely legal in Canada. Bell Fibe TV, Rogers Ignite TV, and Telus Optik TV are all IPTV services operated by Canada's largest companies. The CRTC regulates broadcasting in Canada but does not prohibit consumers from subscribing to IPTV services.

The key distinction is between licensed IPTV services (like NorthStream, which operates transparently with real customer support and accepts Interac e-Transfer) and unlicensed piracy operations (anonymous, crypto-only, no support).
