📌 Quick Verdict

Netflix and IPTV serve different needs — most Canadians should use both. Netflix excels for exclusive series (Stranger Things, The Crown). IPTV is essential for live TV, Canadian channels (CBC, CTV, TSN), and sports (NHL, NFL, NBA). Combined cost: $22-34/month for both vs $120-160/month for cable TV alone.

The Key Difference

Netflix is a streaming service — you watch content on-demand, whenever you want. It has no live TV, no news, and no sports. IPTV is internet television — it replaces cable TV with live channels, real-time sports, and news, plus a massive on-demand library.

They're not really competitors — they're complementary. The question Canadians should be asking is not "Netflix OR IPTV" but "Netflix AND IPTV instead of cable".

When to Choose Each

  • Choose IPTV if you watch live news, follow hockey or football, want CBC, CTV, TSN, RDS, or care about Canadian channels and local content.
  • Choose Netflix if you mainly watch their exclusive original series and don't care about live TV or sports.
  • Use both if you want the complete entertainment package — live TV + on-demand originals — at a fraction of cable's cost.

The Smart Canadian Setup (2026)

Cancel cable TV → Subscribe to NorthStream IPTV ($12.99/mo) + Netflix Standard ($16.99/mo) = $29.98/month total for everything. Compared to Rogers or Bell cable at $120-160/month, you save $1,080-1,560/year while getting more content, better quality, and no contracts.

Is IPTV better than Netflix in Canada?+
They serve different purposes, so comparing them directly isn't quite right. IPTV is better for live TV, sports, and Canadian channels. Netflix is better for exclusive original series. Most Canadian households benefit from having both — the combined cost ($29-35/month) is still far less than cable alone ($120-160/month).
Can I use IPTV and Netflix at the same time?+
Yes — they're completely independent services that both run on your internet connection. You can switch between the NorthStream IPTV app and Netflix at any time on your Firestick, Smart TV, or phone. Many Canadian households use NorthStream for live TV and sports, and Netflix for evenings when they want to binge a series.
